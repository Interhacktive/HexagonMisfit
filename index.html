How We Work

From brief to live

We've delivered installations for keynote stages, permanent public art commissions, live performance tours, and museum openings. Here's how we make all of them work.

01

Discover

We learn your campaign goals, venue constraints, budget range, and audience expectations in one focused brief intake. No endless back-and-forth.

02

Design

You get concept renders, a technical specification, and a production timeline before anything gets built. You can see exactly what we're building before a single part is ordered.

03

Build

Fabrication and software development happen in our studio with full progress visibility for your team. We flag issues early — never at load-in.

04

Deliver

On-site installation, show-day operation support, and post-event documentation you can use for reporting, press, or handoff to an internal team.

About

The studio behind the technology

Hexagon Misfit is a creative technology studio that engineers immersive experiences from concept to installation. We work where software, hardware, and physical fabrication meet — building systems that no single discipline alone could deliver.

Over more than a decade, we have delivered interactive installations across the US and UK — from a 4-story LED chandelier narrating a Mayan folktale, to real-time biometric visualizations on stage at CES with Intel, to the largest projection mapping Seattle has ever seen.

When Intel needs a centerpiece for their CES keynote, when a city commissions its first smart sculpture, when an artist wants their music to drive a room full of light — they call us.
