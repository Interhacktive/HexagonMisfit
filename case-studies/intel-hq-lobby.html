The Brief
"Build something in our lobby that makes every visitor understand, immediately, that they are standing inside a technology company — without anyone telling them."

Intel needed their Santa Clara headquarters to embody their identity as a technology leader from the moment a visitor walked through the door. The lobby had to communicate innovation to partners, employees, and press alike — continuously, without an operator.

Working with All of it Now and Prefrontal Cortex, we designed and built a two-part permanent installation: a large-format Brand Story Wall driven by RealSense depth cameras, and an inverted 360° LED ceiling skylight. Both systems were previewed in VR before a single panel was installed.

Scope of Work

  • VR prototype for pre-build client sign-off
  • Intel RealSense depth camera integration
  • Particle simulation engine (visitor silhouette tracking)
  • Custom LED ceiling skylight fabrication
  • Hardened 24/7 architecture with automatic error recovery
  • Six months post-launch on-call support
The Challenge

Getting client sign-off on a permanent installation before anything was built.

Intel needed to approve a large-scale, permanent installation across two major architectural features — without being able to visualize it in place. Traditional renderings and mockups weren't sufficient for a project of this complexity and budget.

The technical challenge was equally demanding: the Brand Story Wall had to track every visitor who entered the lobby, generate real-time particle simulations around their silhouettes, and do so without a single operator — every hour, every day, indefinitely.

Our Approach

Four phases from brief to permanent installation.

01 — Pre-Build Visualization

VR Prototype

We streamed live Intel RealSense depth data into a virtual model of the lobby, letting Intel stakeholders walk through the finished installation in VR before fabrication began. This was the deciding factor in getting project approval.

02 — Sensor Integration

Depth Camera Pipeline

A multi-camera RealSense D455 array was configured to track visitor silhouettes across the full width of the lobby. The depth data fed directly into a custom particle simulation pipeline built in TouchDesigner.

03 — LED Ceiling Fabrication

Inverted Skylight

Custom LED panels were fabricated and installed as an inverted ceiling skylight, rendering synchronized 360° environments — a seamless visual extension of the Brand Story Wall that fills the peripheral field of view.

04 — Hardened Architecture

24/7 Autonomous Operation

The entire system was built on a hardened architecture with automatic error recovery — designed to self-heal without operator intervention. Hexagon Misfit provided 24/7 on-call support for six months post-completion.

The Result

Zero downtime since launch — and a lobby that makes every visitor stop.

The installation has run continuously since launch with zero downtime. Intel's internal team took over full operations after the six-month post-launch support period. The piece has been featured in Intel's own marketing materials and is shown to every prospective partner who visits the campus.

"The VR prototype was the moment the client understood what we were building. After that walkthrough, there were no more questions."
