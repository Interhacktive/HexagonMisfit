The Brief
"Honor the indigenous communities of the Pacific Northwest through a public light installation that tens of thousands could experience — on one of Seattle's most iconic facades."

The City of Seattle commissioned Hexagon Misfit to create a monumental projection mapping installation on Pier 86 — a landmark building with one of the highest-visibility facades in the city. The work needed to be historically sensitive, technically precise, and powerful enough to hold the attention of thousands of simultaneous viewers across multiple nights.

Two 30k lumen projectors housed in waterproof casings were deployed to handle the waterfront environment, with content authored in Unity3D and mapped in Notch for real-time geometric precision.

This wasn't a backdrop. It was a story that had to perform at architectural scale, in a public environment, with community leaders present at the opening — and get it right on the first night.

The Challenge

A brief that demanded precision and cultural care in equal measure.

The brief called for a historically sensitive installation that could captivate thousands of viewers while honoring the indigenous communities of the Pacific Northwest — a mandate that required as much cultural responsibility as technical execution.

Pier 86's facade presented significant mapping challenges: irregular surface geometry, ambient waterfront lighting, and atmospheric interference from Puget Sound. Getting the geometric calibration right required three full nights of pre-event rigging and testing before a single frame of content could be reviewed.

Our Approach

Four phases from brief to opening night.

01 — Research & Consultation

Cultural Grounding

Before any content was created, we engaged with cultural advisors from the region's tribal nations. Ken Workman, a direct descendant of Chief Seattle, served as the primary cultural consultant — reviewing every motif, color palette, and narrative choice for accuracy and respect.

02 — Surface Mapping

Precision Geometric Calibration

A detailed 3D scan of the Pier 86 facade was used to build a precise mapping mesh in Notch. Content was authored in Unity3D and rendered in real time, allowing for on-site adjustments without re-rendering.

03 — Content Creation

Narrative & Visual Authoring

We developed a multi-chapter narrative — each chapter a distinct visual act depicting waterways, tribal motifs, and ceremonial imagery — rendered in Notch with hand-illustrated source artwork and original sound design.

04 — Installation & Operation

Three Nights of Live Operation

A custom projection rig was rigged and aligned over three nights before the public reveal. During the event run, our team operated the system live — adapting to ambient conditions and ensuring flawless delivery for the opening ceremony.

The Result

Seattle's largest projection mapping drew tens of thousands across its run.

The installation ran across multiple nights to tens of thousands of viewers — the largest projection mapping Seattle had ever seen. Community leaders from several tribal nations participated in the opening ceremony, with the work receiving widespread media coverage as a landmark cultural event.

Ken Workman, descendant of Chief Seattle, was present at the opening ceremony — a testament to the cultural sensitivity and respect embedded throughout the project.

"Community leaders from several tribal nations were present at the opening ceremony. That doesn't happen when you get it wrong."
